HEDGEHOG CAKE

Cake
  • 3¾ cups (470g) Gluten-Free Baking Flour
  • 2½ cups (500g) Caster Sugar
  • 3½ tsp Baking Powder
  • 1 tsp Baking Soda
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • ½ cup (115g) Softened Butter
  • 1½ cups (355ml) Milk
  • 100ml Vegetable Oil
  • 5 Large Eggs
  • 2½ tsp Vanilla Extract
  • Flavouring, if desired
Buttercream
  • 350g Softened Butter
  • 700g Sifted Icing Sugar
  • Flavouring, if desired
Decoration
  • Fondant (assorted colours)
  • Leftover Buttercream
  1. Preheat over to 180°C and grease cake pans with butter.
  2. Whisk together gluten-free flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl.
  3. Add softened butter. Mix, on medium speed, until butter is incorporated.
  4. Add the milk, oil, eggs, vanilla extract, and flavouring (if desired). Continue mixing until batter is smooth and fluffy (about 1 miniute).
  5. Divide batter evenly into prepared pans and bake until a cake tester inserted in the center comes out clean (about 30 minutes).
  6. Cool cake in pan or covered in refrigerator/freezer.
  1. Mix the butter in a medium bowl, on low speed to begin with, then switch to the highest speed and mix for 5 minutes until fluffy and pale in colour.
  2. Stop the mixer and scrape down sides of bowl. Add the icing sugar and resume mixing on low speed.
  3. Once sugar is incorporated, mix on high speed for 5-6 minutes until the mixture becomes pale and fluffy again.
  4. Add flavouring if desired.
  1. Stack and fill cooled cakes with buttercream. Retain some buttercream for decorating.
  2. Carve edges of stacked cake into hedgehog shape.
  1. Use fondant and remaining butercream to cover the cake and add details such as face, arms and spikes.
  2. Construct fondant candle and birthday hat and attach to cake.
